# Security Policy

## Reporting a vulnerability

If you have found a security issue in an Advent Cybersecurity project, please report it privately rather than filing a public issue.

### Preferred: GitHub Private Vulnerability Reporting

Open the affected repository's "Security" tab and click "Report a vulnerability". This is the fastest path to maintainers and keeps the report private until disclosure is coordinated.

### Alternative: email

Contact us via the form at [adventcybersecurity.com/contact](https://www.adventcybersecurity.com/contact) and mark the message as security-related.

### Please include

- Description of the issue and its security impact
- Steps to reproduce, ideally with a minimal proof of concept
- Affected version or commit
- Your name and contact preference for credit (or "anonymous")

## What to expect

- Acknowledgement within 5 business days
- Initial severity and validity assessment within 10 business days
- Coordinated disclosure with credit in the published advisory unless you request anonymity

## Scope

This policy applies to open-source projects published under the [Advent-Cybersecurity](https://github.com/Advent-Cybersecurity) GitHub organization. Issues with our commercial products such as [Breachwright](https://www.adventcybersecurity.com/software/breachwright), or with [adventcybersecurity.com](https://www.adventcybersecurity.com), should be reported via the website contact form rather than GitHub.

## Bug bounty

We do not currently offer a paid bounty. We do offer public credit in security advisories and a written acknowledgement on request.

## Safe harbor

We will not pursue legal action against good-faith security research that follows this policy. Please do not access data that is not yours, do not test systems you do not own without permission, and provide us a reasonable window (typically 90 days) to remediate before public disclosure.
